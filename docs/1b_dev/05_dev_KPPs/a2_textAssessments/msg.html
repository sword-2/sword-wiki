Substitute teacher this day (S.S.)

The message paraphrase is a version to not use at all. This paraphrase is one to steer clear of. It is being used by some evangelicals.

There's translations like the NIV - if its 84, its good; but afterward it is different. The message paraphrase doesn't have another version that's good. If we see other believers are using the message, give them information [warning / caution]. The teacher knows some believers that had the message paraphrase and strayed from their theology.

It would be easy to say just don't use it. But the message paraphrase sold up to 20 million copies. Believers and unbelievers support it - a list, not in any particular order:

  • Max Lucado, prominent evangelical
  • J.I. Packer, author of knowing God
  • Bono, member of the band U2
  • Madeleine L'engel, award-winning author of a wrinkle in time

What is it about this version that appeals to the culture? Deut 4:2, 12:32, etc says we are never to add to or take away from scripture. But the writer of the message does both. He has an agenda for doing it. It was written Eugene Peterson, a Presbyterian 62-91, a spiritual theology professor in British Columbia 92-98. He began writing in 1993 and released it [message] in bits and pieces until it was finally completed in 2002. This version is not a translation at all, its a liberal subjective interpretation of what one man sees. If anything it is a bad commentary on scripture. However, it is touted as a bible and translation.

Normally when a translation is commissioned - a huge of conglomerate of scholars and theologians come together and go thru the manuscript to make a translation. But Eugene did it himself and touts that he did it on his own. There are places where he corrects scripture to say what he thinks it should have said. Eugene said the original languages [Hebrew, Greek, Aramaic] were street languages and were hard to read, so he wanted to improve the interpretation.

Review of formal and dynamic equivalence. Formal doesn't allow the translator to interject their own ideas. A dynamic is thought-for-thought to make it understandable, but sometimes the message doesn't fall under either, but is a paraphrase. But it is advertised as [dynamic equivalence].

A Quote from Eugene about why he wrote it.
While I was teaching a class on Galatians, I began to realize that the adults in my class weren't feeling the vitality and directness that I sensed ...
     [skipping ahead to the key:]
     I hoped to bring the new testament to life for two different types of people: those who hadn't read the bible because it seemed to distant and irrelevant and those who had read the bible so much that it had become 'old hat.

Heb 4:12 says the word of God is living and active, so it is arrogant to say the bible is old hat.

[A quote from Eugene: I realized that I could do it [new testament] at that time. But personally, I didn't think I could do it. Maybe I could do Paul, because Paul is easy in a sense because he gets tangled up and you can untangle him. But I didn't think I could ever do the gospels. I agreed to do ten chapters of Matthew at which time John [NavPress editor] would show it to his colleagues to see what they thought. The first few chapters were really bad, I was just plodding along. But when I hit chapter five, the Sermon on the Mount, something kicked in and I suddenly had that sense of "this is what I do". It was then that I thought I was onto something.]

Here's a man who said: The bible to a lot of people is old hat and needs help - I'm the guy who's going to help it. Some verse by verse comparisons [brief quantity since it would take days to do a lot of them].
