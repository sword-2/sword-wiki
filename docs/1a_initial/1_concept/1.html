Goals

Tables a1-a4 capture some simple goals. The name for each goal group/table are a1 - scripture distribution, a2 - teaching aids, a3 - church business, and a4 - stewardship. The majority of a3/church business is mostly more applications for modules even if the module is just a simple generic book (genbook).

Columns were added that sometimes identify what may be considered unique or partly unique with footnotes at the end of each table. Individual contributors may have varying goals which might have been missed.

Table a1 - scripture distribution
No Unique Name Existing/Consideration Comment(s)/Reference(s)
a1-1 *1 distribute scripture to as many people within a domain as possible-electronic Existing Users and operators should be able to study the bible without making compromises. That includes not having to compromise by using a platform other than their favorite.
a1-2 *2 distribute scripture to as many people within a domain as possible-paper Consideration Some people don't like or may not have an electronic devices. Paper printouts may help some in this group. After an hour or so of troubleshooting errors on their electronic device, where the errors may or may not be from SWORD software, these users can become tired and want to give up on the device and instead get a paper bible.
a1-3 - Bible Text file format Existing An existing SWORD module and file formats correspond to storage of the scriptures in SWORD - OSIS/XML, THML, etc.
  • *1 - Only unique relative to organizations not developing bible software and to software that is not multi-platform. Unique computer interfaces (HCI) for different companies.
  • *2 - A spot check of mobile apps and web sites has not encountered much for generating paper bibles. Some large files were observed that might be printed, but are not for dual use with a bible app.
Teaching aids use the concept that computer memory can aid human memory (reference PSCF #3). Teaching aids that can be accessed quickly and easily may be recycled into the present and not be forgotten.
